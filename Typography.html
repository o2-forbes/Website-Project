    • Typography is the art and technique of arranging and designing text to make it visually appealing while also retaining its eligibility to be read. It involves selecting typefaces, point sizes, line lengths, line-spacing, and letter-spacing, as well as adjusting the overall layout and its variety of text elements. Typography is a crucial aspect of graphic design, publishing, and various other types of visual communication and art.

    As stated in

    Axial designs consist of lines of text positioned on either side of a line. They can be either symmetrical or asymmetrical around their axis and the lines can be any rotation and scale.

    Bilateral designs include text that is formatted in the centre style. It can be positioned on alternating areas of the canvas but must be in the style stated before.

    Dilatational designs conist of lines of text that are set along different circular paths that bend and curve across the canvas either around a point or a shape.
